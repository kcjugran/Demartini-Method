The Demartini Method

A space to bring a charged perception back into balance

How to use this method

Purpose

Every emotion is a ratio of perceptions. When you perceive more upside than downside you become infatuated (or proud). More downside than upside, and you become resentful (or guilty). The Method gathers the missing side until the perception is balanced — and a balanced perception releases the charge into gratitude and presence.

Choose your focus

Another person — a charge toward someone else (resentment or infatuation).
Myself — a charge toward your own conduct (guilt/shame or pride/arrogance).
An event — a charge toward a situation with no single human cause.
A loss or change — a perceived loss or gain through change (Side C).

Step 1 — Identify the TAI

List the top 3 highest-priority Traits, Actions or Inactions. A TAI is observable in 3–4 words. Exclude feelings (loving, humiliated), synonyms (nice, mean), and labels (good person, abusive). Pinpoint where, when, and to whom.

Step 2 — Work each column, per item

Each column has one box per item. Answer fully for each before moving on. Quantity matters — keep adding real, specific moments until both sides equalise. Use all Seven Areas and move through time (past → present).

Step 3 — The balancing logic

Reflection: you (or others) own the same TAI equally. Drawbacks/Benefits: the charged trait carries the opposite value too. Opposite to the same person: dissolves 'always/never' labels. Synchronicity: someone provided the opposite at that very moment. Antidote: had it gone the other way, consequences would equalise.

Step 4 — Completion

The charge dissolves into felt gratitude — often a quiet settling, moist eyes, a soft 'thank you'. If a pull remains, there are more answers to gather. Balance, not suppression, is the destination.
